9/18/23 - For our Rube Goldberg machine, we made some minor fixes to our stability of our design, such as cardboard triangles and added extra tape to the side of our project. We noticed that the machine was leaning to the left, so we added carboard triangles to balance it. To satisfy criteria and to make the building process easier, we removed the pulley and added a ramp to create more force for our car to knock the baseball down. After these adjustments, we tested our machine and it worked successfully. At this time, I have added the video for the successful attempt for our project.

10/4/23 - Today, we were tasked to make prosthetic hand out of cardboard, string, and tape and pick up a plastic water bottle. David, Marc, and I were able to successfully build a functional hand for short period of time. O our first attempt, the design did ot work, but with the string, we were able to make mechanism where the string would pull the carboard fingers into a grip position. We also added tape to the tips of the fingers for extra grip. With those mdifications, we were able to pick the water bottle up.
